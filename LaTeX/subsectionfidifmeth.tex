The finite difference method employs a Taylor series in order to solve a partial differential equation like equation \eqref{eq:richeq_3D} \parencite{fornberg_finite_2011}.  Truncating the Taylor series given by equation \eqref{eq:tayse} after the first derivative (i.e., $n = 1$) gives
\begin{align*}
  f(x) &= \frac{(x - x_0)^0}{0!}f^{(0)}(x_0) + \frac{(x - x_0)^1}{1!}f^{(1)}(x_0) + R_1 \\
       &= f(x_0) + (x - x_0)f'(x_0) + R_1
\end{align*}
Solving for $f'(x_0)$ gives
\begin{align*}
  f'(x_0) = \frac{f(x)}{x - x_0} - \frac{f(x_0)}{x - x_0} - \frac{R_1}{x - x_0} \, .
\end{align*}
Assuming that $\frac{R_1}{x - x_0}$ is sufficiently small we obtain
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:fidifmeth}
  f'(x_0) \approx \frac{f(x) - f(x_0)}{x - x_0}
\end{equation}
